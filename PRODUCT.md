# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Road travelers planning multi-stop trips: families, RV owners, truck drivers, and everyday drivers who want hotels, food, fuel/EV stops, and routing in one place. They use TripMappa on desktop and mobile while planning at home and on the road — often switching between map view, itinerary results, and live navigation.

## Product Purpose

TripMappa is an AI-powered road trip planner with real map routing, vehicle-specific logic (car, RV, truck, multi-vehicle), and a conversational question flow that produces actionable itineraries. Success means users trust the route, find stops that fit their vehicle and preferences, and can navigate without leaving the app.

## Brand Personality

Warm, capable, and distinctly American-road-trip. Confident without corporate stiffness; nostalgic where it earns it (vintage signage on map popups), modern and clear everywhere else. Voice: helpful co-pilot, not chatbot hype.

Three words: **Warm · Capable · Roadwise**

## Anti-references

- Generic AI SaaS landing pages (Inter-only, purple/violet gradients, card-in-card nesting)
- Gray muted text on saturated colored backgrounds
- Weak hierarchy where every element competes at the same weight
- Over-decorated product UI that slows task completion
- Copying copyrighted Cars/Route 66 IP — Americana *vibe* only, generic vintage motel/diner sign shapes

## Design Principles

1. **Serve the trip, not the chrome** — Map, itinerary, and navigation are primary; decoration supports orientation, never blocks it.
2. **Earned nostalgia** — Retro neon/sign treatment is reserved for moments that reward it (e.g. map marker popups), not every surface.
3. **Gold-and-violet identity, not template purple** — Accent with TripMappa gold and orange on deep violet night surfaces; avoid default AI gradient palettes.
4. **Hierarchy before decoration** — One clear primary action per screen; labels, data, and actions must scan in order of importance.
5. **Practice what we preach on the road** — Planning and navigating should feel as reliable as the best category tools (maps, booking, routing), with TripMappa's own warmth.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA where practical for core flows (planning, results, navigation, auth)
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for animations and sky-cycle transitions
- Ensure focus states and contrast on night-mode violet backgrounds (gold/orange accents must remain readable)
- Do not rely on color alone for stop category meaning — pair with labels and shape where possible
